AI & Development

Software people actually want to use

I'm Taylor Akin — a developer and technical lead who specializes in the gap most software falls into: the space between what people say they need and what would actually help them. My best work starts in a conversation, not an IDE.

How I work

Every project, the same spine

  1. Listen first. I sit with the people who live in the process — the nontechnical users, the frustrated stakeholders — and map what's really happening, not what the ticket says.
  2. Translate. Fuzzy needs become concrete requirements. Expectations get set early and managed honestly — no surprise gaps at delivery.
  3. Design for the human. UX that feels intuitive isn't decoration; it's whether the tool gets used at all.
  4. Automate the boring parts. Increasingly with AI — the goal is always to give people their time back for the work that needs a human.

Case study

Custom reporting at scale — from conversation to delivered solution

For over two years I built custom report solutions on PHP-based frameworks for payroll, time and attendance, deductions, and allocations data. The code was the easy half. The real craft was the full arc: gathering specs from nontechnical clients, managing scope and expectations, designing outputs people could actually read, and executing delivery — plus documentation for complex setups that directly contributed to saving client relationships.

As a team lead, I treated the team itself as a system to design. I built dashboards for visibility, an automated ticketing pipeline (Microsoft Forms → Power Automate → Planner), automated quality gates, and unit-testing and root-cause-analysis protocols — the infrastructure that lets a team move faster and break less.
