Consulting & Leadership

Better systems. Better teams. Same conversation.

Most process problems are people problems wearing a trench coat. I've led development teams through exactly the transitions many organizations are staring down right now — adopting AI without torching quality, fixing processes without steamrolling the people in them, and turning "the requirements are unclear" from a complaint into a solvable problem.

Ways we can work together

Three shapes of engagement

Process & AI adoption

Hands-on help automating manual work and building AI-assisted workflows your team will actually trust — including the quality systems (testing, review gates, RCA protocols) that make AI acceleration safe instead of scary.

Fractional technical leadership

Part-time technical lead or advisor for teams that need senior judgment — architecture review, expectation management, translating between stakeholders and developers — without a full-time hire.

Workshops & speaking

Working sessions and talks on AI-assisted development, quality systems, and servant leadership — grounded in what I've actually run, not slideware.

The track record

Done, not theorized

  1. Led a team of 5–7 developers across two offices as Team Lead for Custom Report Development.
  2. Built the quality infrastructure — unit testing standards, post-mortem/RCA protocols, automated quality gates — that let the team move faster and break less.
  3. Onboarded and mentored multiple developers; served as primary trainer for new hires.
  4. Now a Technical Lead supporting cross-functional partnerships across an AI-powered data automation portfolio.
How I lead

People first, systems second — in that order

I lead as a servant leader: my job is to develop people and build systems that scale. The influences are on my reading list — Extreme Ownership, Crucial Conversations, The Coaching Habit — and the practice is backed by ongoing development: the PULSE Leadership Summit, CliftonStrengths, and Emotional Intelligence certifications.

The through-line with everything else on this site: improve the process without losing sight of the people using it.
